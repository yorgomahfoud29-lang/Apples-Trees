Quatrième de couverture

At thirteen, she wished her father were dead. At sixteen, she is still learning what it means to live.

Between those two ages unfolds a story of ambition, obsession, and survival. Raised to believe she was destined for greatness, she chases perfection with a hunger that never quiets; academically, physically, emotionally, until the pursuit begins to consume her. Beneath the image of the "gifted" girl lies a mind at war with itself, torn between self-love and self-destruction.

As she navigates toxic friendships, blurred boundaries, and fragile first loves, she learns how easily affection can turn into control, and how quickly innocence can be rewritten by experience. From the quiet violence inside her home to the louder chaos of the world outside it, she is forced to confront betrayal, fear, and the weight of growing up too fast.

But this is not just a story of pain. It is a story of awareness. Of reclaiming control. Of learning that healing does not arrive all at once, but in fragments, through strength, through distance, through love that is finally gentle.

Raw, introspective, and unflinchingly honest, this memoir traces the making of a girl who refuses to be defined by what tried to break her and who dares to believe that cycles can end, that identity can be rebuilt, and that the future is still hers to shape.

Apples & Trees — Leana Khoury, 2026
